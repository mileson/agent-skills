# Anthropic Brand Guidelines for Image Generation

This file defines the core visual identity of the Anthropic brand, specifically optimized for injection into AI image generation prompts.

## Core Identity
Anthropic's visual style is characterized by a warm, intellectual, and understated elegance. It avoids neon, hyper-vibrant, or overly "tech/cyberpunk" aesthetics. The focus is on clarity, humanity, and a subtle mid-century or editorial feel.

## Color Palette (To be injected into prompts)

When this brand is applied to image generation, use the following hex codes and color descriptions in the prompt:

**Primary Background & Canvas Colors:**
- `#faf9f5` (Warm off-white / Alabaster) - Use for clean, bright backgrounds.
- `#e8e6dc` (Subtle light beige / Putty) - Use for secondary backgrounds or soft shadows.
- `#141413` (Soft black / Deep charcoal) - Use for primary dark backgrounds, strong text, or high-contrast structural elements.

**Accent Colors (For shapes, highlights, and conceptual elements):**
- `#d97757` (Earthy terracotta orange / Burnt coral) - Primary accent for warmth and energy.
- `#6a9bcc` (Muted steel blue / Slate blue) - Secondary accent for trust, structure, and calm.
- `#788c5d` (Sage green / Olive) - Tertiary accent for balance and growth.
- `#b0aea5` (Warm mid-gray) - For structural lines, borders, or secondary information.

## Prompt Injection Template

When an Agent needs to inject this brand into the `{brand_guidelines}` placeholder, use the following descriptive text:

```text
color palette strictly featuring background #faf9f5, off-white #e8e6dc, dark charcoal #141413, accented with terracotta orange #d97757, slate blue #6a9bcc, and sage green #788c5d, elegant intellectual corporate aesthetic, warm and understated editorial style, no neon colors, balanced composition
```

## Usage Rules
- Do NOT use pure white (`#FFFFFF`) or pure black (`#000000`).
- Maintain a matte or slightly grainy texture; avoid high-gloss, plastic, or overly metallic 3D renders unless specifically requested by the user.
- Emphasize negative space (white space) to let the structure breathe.
